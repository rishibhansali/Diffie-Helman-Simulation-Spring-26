Diffie-Hellman Key Exchange

The baseline protocol: Alice and Bob establish a shared secret over a completely public channel without ever transmitting it. The security rests on the computational hardness of the Discrete Logarithm Problem.

Step-by-Step Breakdown

1

Agree on Public Parameters

Alice and Bob publicly agree on a large prime p and a primitive root (generator) g. Anyone can know these — they provide no advantage to an eavesdropper.

2

Alice Generates Her Key Pair

Alice picks a secret random integer X_A and computes her public key Y_A = g^X_A mod p. She sends Y_A to Bob over the public channel.

3

Bob Generates His Key Pair

Bob similarly picks a secret X_B and computes Y_B = g^X_B mod p. He sends Y_B to Alice over the public channel.

4

Both Compute the Shared Secret

Alice computes K = Y_B^X_A mod p. Bob computes K = Y_A^X_B mod p. By the properties of modular exponentiation, both values are identical — this is the shared secret.

5

Security Analysis

An eavesdropper sees p, g, Y_A, and Y_B — but to find X_A or X_B they must solve the Discrete Logarithm Problem (DLP): find x such that g^x ≡ Y (mod p). For a 2048-bit prime, this is computationally infeasible with any known algorithm.
