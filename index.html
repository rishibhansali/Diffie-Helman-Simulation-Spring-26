Simulation Modules

🔑
Baseline

DH Key Exchange

The canonical Diffie-Hellman handshake between Alice and Bob. Watch public keys travel, private keys stay hidden, and both parties arrive at the same shared secret.
👁
Attack

MITM Attack

Eve positions herself between Alice and Bob, intercepting and replacing public keys. Neither party suspects a thing — and Eve can read every message.
💀
Attack

Weak Prime Attack

Choose a small prime and watch the brute-force discrete logarithm attack crack the private key in real time. Compare to the infeasibility of attacking 2048-bit primes.
🔒
Defense

Forward Secrecy

What happens when a long-term key is leaked? With static keys, all past sessions are exposed. Ephemeral keys guarantee past sessions stay secret even after compromise.
✅
Defense

Authenticated DH

The MITM attack relies on anonymity. Add digital signatures to public keys and Eve's interception fails instantly — identity verification closes the gap.

How Diffie-Hellman Works

Step 01

Public Parameters

Agree on p, g

Alice and Bob publicly agree on a large prime p and a generator g. These can be known to everyone.

Step 02

Private Keys

X_A, X_B ← secret

Each party picks a secret private key at random. These never leave their machine.

Step 03

Public Keys

Y_A = g^X_A mod p

Each party computes a public key using modular exponentiation and broadcasts it.

Step 04

Shared Secret

K = Y_B^X_A mod p

Alice raises Bob's public key to her private key. Bob does the same with Alice's key. Both get identical K.

Step 05

Security Basis

DLP: g^x ≡ Y (mod p)

An attacker knowing only Y, g, and p must solve the Discrete Logarithm Problem — computationally infeasible for large p.
